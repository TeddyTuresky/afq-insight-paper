Statistical methods in tractometry

The three approaches

Domain agnostic view of tractometry research landscape:

In the first approach, researchers use machine learning techniques to predict clinical diagnoses from brain measures, such as the white matter integrity at all points along an anatomical tract. This approach can achieve high accuracy, but may require extensive feature engineering. It may additionally obscure the importance of particular brain features.

In the second approach, researchers make group comparisons between patients and controls in order to understand the relationship between a disease or disorder and the underlying anatomy. Typically, researchers adopt a mass univariate approach, which lacks the predictive power of approach #1 and can fail to account for feature colinearity.

In this work, we adopt an approach that can predict clinical diagnoses while also revealing important features of the underlying neuroanatomy. It requires no apriori feature selection but instead capitalizes on the known group structure of diffusion MRI data.
